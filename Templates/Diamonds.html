Overview

Our goal was to build a machine learning model to predict if, based on characteristics of a diamond like shape and color, it was grown naturally or produced in a lab. We also wanted to learn the correlation between characteristics and price, to see how a diamond gains value.

Our dataset was collected by Miguel Corral Jr. The data was scraped on Nov. 29, 2020. The data contains eight characteristics of a diamond. We used logistic regression to predict if a diamond is natural or lab grown.

Characteristics explained

Shape describes the geometric appearance of the diamond.

Cut is the symmetry and reflective qualities of the diamond.

Color describes if there's a natural color or lack of color. D is the highest grade possible.

Clarity tells you if there are imperfections or 'inclusions' - a flawless diamond has none. FL is the highest grade possible.

Carat is the weight of the diamond.

Price is the dollar amount, in U.S. dollars, the diamond is worth.

Report is a diamond grading report or certificate from an independent lab.

The model

Initially, our plan was to do a Multiple Linear Regression Model to predict price. However, after running the model, our accuracy was only 0.31. Our next thought was to do a Logistic Regression Model to predict the diamond type (natural or lab-grown). The features we used for the model are shape, cut, color, clarity, carat, price, and report. Our target is the diamond's type, either natural or lab-grown. After running the logistic model, we received an accuracy of 0.98. This is a significant improvement from our Multiple Linear Regresssion Model. From our logistic model, we can predict the type of diamond.

By the end of 2020 the average cost difference between a lab-grown diamond and that if its counterpart differed only about nine percent. So far in 2021 retail pricing for lab-grown diamonds has continued to drop but as much as 10%, however, this is every-changing due to recent issues of consumer demand and supply chain.
And finally, lab-grown diamonds are real diamonds both from a chemical and physical perspective. The naked eye is unable to tell the difference between and lab-grown and natural diamond. One would need special equipment to view trace elements and crystal growth in order to determine the minor differences in each.
